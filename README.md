## About

19+ years in Java. Most recently 13 years at Oracle, as the founding and technical owner of an enterprise component catalog service — a 30-module Jakarta EE platform running in two production environments for 7+ years, serving hundreds of thousands of component versions across multiple product teams' development pipelines.

I work end-to-end: requirements analysis, domain modelling, architecture, implementation, database design, cloud integration, and release. The strongest zone is the middle of the stack — real-time search and indexing on Apache Lucene, pluggable persistence abstractions, custom compile-time CDI frameworks, event-driven integration, and OAuth/JWT security over Jakarta EE. Comfortable shipping the same codebase as independent microservices on Kubernetes or as a modular monolith, depending on what the environment actually needs.

I use frontier AI tooling as a force multiplier where it earns its place, and keep it out of problems where a deterministic solution is the right answer. Most of what people currently try to solve with an LLM is really a better parser, a tuned analyzer, or a correctly-shaped index. Knowing which is which is the interesting part of the job.

## Selected highlights

- Reduced component dependency resolution latency from **tens of seconds to tens of milliseconds** through redesigned algorithms and Lucene-backed indexing.
- Scaled the catalog service to **~200 requests / second per node** with sub-second response times under production load.
- Indexed **~400,000 components in 15 minutes** through optimised Lucene index build pipelines.
- Authored a **custom compile-time CDI framework** that moves DI graph errors from runtime to build time and eliminates reflection overhead — still in production use across the platform.
- Designed a **pluggable storage layer** supporting Oracle DB, filesystem, and REST-based remote catalog federation with consistent transactional semantics across backends.
